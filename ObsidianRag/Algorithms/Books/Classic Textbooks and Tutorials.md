# Algorithms — Classic Textbooks & Tutorials

> Already have: *Introduction to Algorithms* (Cormen, Leiserson, Rivest, Stein — "CLRS") in this folder. Below are other widely-recognized classics worth adding to your reading list.

## Foundational Textbooks

- **"Introduction to Algorithms" (CLRS)** — already in this folder. The standard graduate-level reference; rigorous proofs, broad coverage.
- **"Algorithms" by Robert Sedgewick & Kevin Wayne** — more implementation-oriented, pairs with the free Princeton Coursera course and algs4 Java library.
- **"The Algorithm Design Manual" by Steven Skiena** — practical, war-story driven, excellent for building intuition and picking the right algorithm for a real problem.

## Competitive Programming (matches this vault's topic structure well)

- **"Competitive Programmer's Handbook" by Antti Laaksonen** — free official PDF: https://cses.fi/book/book.pdf — concise coverage of exactly the topics in this folder's subfolders (Data Structures, Graph Theory, DP, Strings, Math).
- **"算法竞赛进阶指南" (Lou Tiancheng)** — Chinese classic bridging fundamentals to advanced competitive topics (matches Miscellaneous/Computational Geometry subfolders here).
- **"挑战程序设计竞赛" (Programming Contest Challenge Book)** — Japanese ICPC classic, translated to Chinese/English, strong on Graph Theory and DP.
- **CP-Algorithms (cp-algorithms.com)** — free online reference wiki, closely mirrors this vault's per-topic note structure.

## Specialized References

- **"Computational Geometry: Algorithms and Applications" by de Berg et al.** — the standard reference for the Computational Geometry subfolder.
- **"Randomized Algorithms" by Motwani & Raghavan** — for Miscellaneous/Simulated Annealing & randomized techniques.

## Suggested use

Use CLRS/Sedgewick for rigorous theory behind each MOC note, and the Competitive Programmer's Handbook or 算法竞赛进阶指南 as a faster practical companion when preparing for contests.
